Slippy Map

The slippy map is an Ajax component. JavaScript runs in the browser, which dynamically requests maps from a server in the background (without reloading the whole HTML page) to give a smooth slippy zoomy map browsing experience.

Health Data

Data gathered by the Iowa Department of Public Health was analyized in ArcMap and visualized in Carto. The purpose of this was to see if there was a correlation between the number of smokers, the age of the popualtion, and the number of dealths per county and the cost of health care. All data is per capita.

Iowa Climate Data

Data gathered by the Iowa Environmental Mesonet were analyzed in ArcMap to produce shapefiles. The shapefiles were then uploaded into Carto to produce the maps displayed in the page that follows. The purpose of these maps was to show if there is a correlation between temperatures in the summer and winter and the amount of precipation for that year.
